I missed that tiny 'i' in GiB for longer than I'd like to admit.

GiB. Not GB.

Sit a little far from your screen and they look identical.

But that one character is doing a lot of work.

When I noticed AWS using three different units in the same console:
• memory in GiB
• storage in GB
• network in Gigabit

My first instinct was that AWS was being inconsistent.

It wasn't.

Each one is accurate. And understanding why took me through:
• why RAM has no choice but to be binary
• why storage manufacturers picked base 10 in the 1980s (and it wasn't engineering)
• why your OS shows 931 GB for a 1TB drive, and why nobody fixed the label
• and the one OS that actually did fix it

Once it clicked, the AWS console stopped looking inconsistent and started looking precise.
